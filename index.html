John Monash:
The Australian Icon

John Monash is one of the most recognisable contemporary icons. He served Australia as a soldier, an engineer and a commissioner. Despite this status, his actions and ideas are not often discussed nor widely known outside of his military career.

This website aims to explore the ways that Monash has influenced Australia through four perspectives, at four points of his life, and in four key turning points in Australian history. The interactive timeline below briefly covers key events in Monash's life. Click on the four major turning points to learn more about these asects of his life.

27th of June, 1865 - Turning Point: John Monash is born to Polish-German Australians Louis Monash and Bertha nee Manasse. He is born in West Melbourne and is the only son of his Jewish parents. Click here to read more about his heritage.

4th July 1918 - Turning Point: John Monash leads the battle of Hamel, resulting in a decisive win for the allies through his innovative tactics. (Click here to find out more)
8th August 1918 - John Monash leads the battle of Amiens, gaining 21km of land for the allies.
12th August 1918 - King George V knights John Monash for his efforts in the great war.
late 1920 - Turning Point: Victoria is in growing demand for electric power. Monash is appointed chairman of the State Electricity Commission, which seeks to meet these demands where businesses are not. The commission's solution is to launch the largest coordinated state infrastructure program at the time, and Monash is tasked with spearheading this movement. (Click here to find out More)
1931 - Turning Point: Monash dies. His funeral is the largest in Australia with over 30 000 attendees.
