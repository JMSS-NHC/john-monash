Monash - An Australian Icon

The Life of Monash in becoming an Icon and Figure of Respect

John Monash is truly a strongly revered and loved Australian icon. His contributions as a civil engineer and Australian war hero are more than significant to Australia. The presence and very name of “John Monash” holds power and respect. The values of John Monash was to pose and contribute to the community. The contributions of John Monash to Australia were and still are admired by many. The funeral held for John Monash in 1931 was accompanied by around 300,000 mourners (The Barrier Miner, 1931) is indicative of his significance and importance. 300,000 people is a tremendous number and would have been around 30% of Melbourne city and 15% of the state of Victoria in attendance during 1931.

The legacies of John Monash has been left deeply engraved in Australia’s past and contemporary history. Years after Sir John Monash’s passing, he still is consistently mentioned and his name has influence in many parts of Australia. Several eponyms for John Monash are prevalent in Australia, notably Monash University, Monash Medical Centre, etc. The numerous commemorations under his name demonstrates his impact and influence to Australia.
